Understanding CO₂ Emissions - Why They Matter

Carbon dioxide (CO₂) is the most significant greenhouse gas released by human activity, primarily through the burning of fossil fuels, industrial processes, and deforestation. As CO₂ accumulates in the atmosphere, it traps heat and accelerates global warming. The consequences are far-reaching: rising sea levels, extreme weather events, loss of biodiversity, and growing threats to food and water security. Tracking CO₂ emissions is not just a scientific task — it is a societal necessity. By understanding who emits how much, where, and why, we can hold governments and corporations accountable and develop more effective strategies for climate protection. Transparent, data-driven insights are key to reducing our carbon footprint and reaching international climate goals like those set in the Paris Agreement. This platform provides an overview of global and corporate CO₂ emissions to inform users, raise awareness, and support action. Whether you're a student, policymaker, activist, or simply curious, understanding CO₂ emissions is the first step toward a more sustainable future.
